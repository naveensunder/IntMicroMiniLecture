How Criminals Make Decisions: A Microeconomic Perspective

A curated set of readings, podcasts, and videos to accompany today's lecture. Selected for accessibility — no prior economics knowledge required. Each item includes an estimated time and a note on how it connects to what we covered.

⏱ ~2.5 hrs total material · 3 articles  ·  3 podcasts  ·  3 videos
About this lecture. Today's session introduced game theory — the branch of economics that models strategic decisions where your best choice depends on what others do. We covered the Prisoner's Dilemma, Nash Equilibrium, sequential games, and backward induction, with applications to AI company competition, antitrust, and business strategy.
